# SolShot Pitch Video — 2-min Zoom Conversation (v4)

**Format:** Zoom call, both with cameras OFF (PFPs visible in the thumbnail strip), one person screen-shares solshot.gg with a vs Shot Bot match running quietly in the background. Audio is the Q&A conversation. Hard 2-min cap, aim 1:55.

**Why no faces:** the demo video already has Fish's voiceover over 3 mins of structured product walkthrough. Two video formats with talking-heads on top would be redundant. Pitch differentiates by being conversational + audio-led, with ambient gameplay as the visual layer.

**Why screen-share over static PFPs:** two static profile pictures for 2 minutes reads as low effort. A casual vs Shot Bot match running in the background provides visual interest without competing for attention — the audio Q&A is still the substance.

**Brief from Colosseum (verbatim):**
> "Separate from the demo video — introduce yourselves, tell us what you're building, and tell us why you're the people to build it. Nothing fancy required. **We're interested in how you think and communicate.** YouTube, Loom, or Vimeo. Up to 2 minutes."

**v3 reframe (2026-05-11):** JJ + Fish aligned on a conversational Q&A pattern. Trade questions to land 4 anchors naturally instead of taking turns reciting beats. Reads like two friends figuring something out together, not a pitch.

## The 4 anchors

Everything has to land these by the time the video ends:

| Anchor | Fish's framing | What it really means |
|---|---|---|
| **Why this?** | Group chats are where attention lives now | Crypto-native multiplayer wagering belongs inside the chats where the audience already hangs out, not in another app to download |
| **Why now?** | Dry markets, nostalgia, semi-idle attention spans | Bear-market quiet means people aren't chasing the next memecoin — they're back to wanting to play. Skill games scratch that itch. Async-turn cadence fits their bandwidth. |
| **Why us?** | Gamers, degens, love for the space — and we see the gap others can't | We're not coming at this as crypto-natives trying to bolt on games, or game devs trying to bolt on crypto. We've lived in both. The gap is visible to us in a way it isn't to either pure tribe. |
| **Where next?** | Monopolise the gap. Not just SolShot — a whole group-chat arcade | Same backend, multiple games (golf, 8-ball, basketball, darts), then multi-platform (Seekr Mobile, iMessage, WhatsApp). One shared SHOT economy. |

## Beat sheet (2:00 hard cap)

### Beat 1 — Natural intros (0:00 → 0:20)

**JJ leads. Fish picks up.** No script, no formal "we're here today to..." opener. Just hi, name, who you are, what you do on SolShot. Two sentences each, maximum.

Don't say "co-founder" or "founder" — too pitch-deck. Say what you actually do. ("I build the thing." / "I work on the product and the story.")

The handoff between you should feel like a regular Zoom call — eye contact across the screen, small nod, the other one picks up.

### Beat 2 — JJ asks Fish: "So why do YOU think we built this?" (0:20 → 0:55)

**Delivery:** casual, almost jokey. The "lol" energy JJ floated in chat. The question is deliberately undersold so the answer doesn't sound rehearsed.

**Fish lands two anchors in one go (the natural pairing):**

- **Why this** — "Group chats are where everyone hangs out now. That's where people are spending their time. Nobody's built a wagered skill game inside the chat itself — they've all built another app you have to leave the chat to play."
- **Why now** — "And the timing matters. Markets have been quiet for a minute. Attention spans are shot. People are nostalgic for the skill games we grew up on. The cadence of a Pocket Tanks turn fits exactly into how a group chat actually works."

This is a 2-for-1 because they're naturally tied. Don't separate them with a "and now let me address the timing" beat — let them flow.

JJ reacts in real-time. Nods. "Yeah." "Right." Stays alive on camera.

### Beat 3 — Fish asks back: "And why us?" (0:55 → 1:30)

**Fish flips it.** Short prompt: *"And why us, then? Why are we the ones building this?"*

**JJ lands "why us":**
- Lifelong gamer. Grew up on the artillery genre — Pocket Tanks, Worms, Scorched Earth.
- Lived in the trench/whale side of crypto — knows that audience because they're the people in the group chats every day.
- Fish brings the product brain and the design eye; JJ does the shipping.
- "We see the gap others can't — gaming people don't speak crypto, crypto people don't speak gaming. We do both."

Optional anchor line if it lands naturally: *"We're not pitching what we'd build if you funded us. We're pitching what we already built."*

### Beat 4 — JJ pivots: "So where does this go?" (1:30 → 1:55)

**JJ rolls naturally from his "why us" answer into the vision.** This is where the pitch graduates from "what is" to "what becomes."

**JJ lands "where next":**
- "SolShot's the wedge. Artillery is the first game."
- "The bet is the whole group-chat arcade — golf, 8-ball, basketball, hockey, darts. Same wallet, same SHOT economy, same async-turn loop."
- "Then we ship the same backend on Seekr Mobile, iMessage, WhatsApp. Adding a chat surface isn't a rebuild. It's an addition."
- *"We want to monopolise the gap we see. Nobody else is building this."*

Fish riffs in with one supporting thought — maybe the moat line:
- *"Single-game wagers compete on game quality. Multi-game async wagers across a friend group — that's infrastructure. That's the moat."*

### Beat 5 — Close (1:55 → 2:00)

Whoever's most recently on point lands it. Direct, short, friendly:

> *"Mainnet's a week or two out. We're applying because we want to skip the part where we slow down to fundraise. SolShot.gg, code's open on GitHub. Thanks for watching."*

Hard out.

## The two questions to land verbatim

The framing of the questions matters more than the answers. If these two land naturally, the conversation works:

1. **JJ to Fish:** *"So why do YOU think we built this?"* (Beat 2 opener — the "lol" energy is key. Undersell it.)
2. **Fish to JJ:** *"And why us, then?"* (Beat 3 opener — short, direct, no preamble.)

Don't overwrite the questions. Improvise the answers.

## Production notes

- **Setup:** Zoom call, gallery view ON, record locally. Loom also works.
- **Lighting:** face a window or lamp. Don't backlight.
- **Audio:** wired headphones with mic > AirPods > laptop mic. Test before.
- **Don't sit silent while the other talks.** Nod, "yeah", react. Frozen face for 30s on camera reads as awkward.
- **Take it twice.** First take is for warm-up, second is the keeper. Don't re-record more than 3 times.
- **Hard 2-min cap.** Edit out silence > 1 second. No music, no B-roll, no graphics — Colosseum said "nothing fancy required" and polish hurts here.
- **Background:** plain wall, no bookshelf flex.

---

## What NOT to do

- **No screenshare.** The demo video covers the product proof.
- **No formal pitch deck** behind you.
- **No memorised speech.** If you sound like you're reading, the format breaks.
- **No "as a founder…" / "we strongly believe…" / VC-deck phrasing.**
- **No buzzword stack.** Talk plain.
- **No questions you haven't briefly thought about.** The Q&A only works if both sides have at least a vague answer ready — but DON'T script the answers, just know the territory.

---

## If you only remember three things

1. **The questions are the format.** "So why do YOU think we built this?" and "And why us, then?" — these two prompts hold the whole conversation together.
2. **2-for-1 pairing on Beat 2.** Fish lands why-this AND why-now in one answer. They're naturally tied. Don't separate.
3. **Beat 4 graduates the pitch.** JJ rolls his "why us" answer into "where next" — same breath. That's how a pitch reads as forward-looking, not retrospective.
