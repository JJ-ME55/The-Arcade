## Beat-by-beat answers

### 1 · INTROS · 0:00 → 0:20

**JJ leads. Fish picks up.** Natural.

- Name + what you actually do on SolShot (one sentence each)
- No "co-founder" / "founder" / "we're a team of two" — too pitch-deck
- Soft handoff. JJ says his bit, glances at Fish, Fish picks up.

---

### 2 · "SO WHY DO YOU THINK WE BUILT THIS?" · 0:20 → 0:55

**JJ asks Fish.** Casual, almost jokey. The "lol" tone. Undersell the question.

> *"So why do YOU think we built this?"*

**Fish lands TWO anchors in ~35 seconds. Don't separate them — they tie naturally.**

**WHY THIS — points to hit:**
- Group chats are the dominant social surface now (TG, iMessage, WhatsApp). That's where the audience already is.
- Crypto-native communities (trench chats, whale groups, alpha groups) live in TG specifically. They're already wagering on memecoins.
- But nobody's built a wagered **skill** game INSIDE the chat itself. Every "TG game" is a separate web app linked from the chat. The chat is the funnel, not the playing field.

**WHY NOW — points to hit:**
- Markets are dry. People who were day-trading attention are bored and looking for something to play.
- Nostalgia for Pocket Tanks / Worms / Scorched Earth — the genre's been dormant for 10 years.
- Async-turn cadence fits how people actually use group chats — between memes and price calls, not a sit-down session.
- Privy + Solana means a new player is in their first wagered match in under a minute. The friction tax that killed earlier crypto games is gone.

**Strong lines to drop if natural:**
- *"Async fits, real-time fights."* (the wedge insight)
- *"Group chats are where attention lives now."*
- *"Nobody's built wagered gaming inside the chat itself — they've all built another app you have to leave the chat to play."*

**What a 35-second natural delivery sounds like (do NOT recite — this is a feel reference):**
> *"Look, group chats are where everyone hangs out now. That's just where the attention is. And nobody's built a wagered skill game that runs INSIDE the chat — they've all built another app you have to leave the chat to play. On timing, markets have been dry for a minute, people are bored, attention spans are shot. The cadence of a Pocket Tanks turn fits exactly into how a group chat actually works. Async fits, real-time fights it."*

JJ nods + reacts in real-time. Doesn't interrupt.

### 3 · "AND WHY US?" · 0:55 → 1:30

**Fish flips it.** Short. No preamble.

> *"And why us, then? Why are we the ones building this?"*

**JJ lands "why us" in ~35 seconds.**

**WHY US — points to hit:**
- Both lifelong gamers. JJ grew up on the artillery genre — Pocket Tanks, Worms, Scorched Earth. Built this from love of the genre, not "let's launch a crypto game."
- Both lived in the trench/whale side of TG crypto. Know the audience because they're in it.
- The crucial gap: gaming people don't speak crypto, crypto people don't speak gaming. SolShot needs both — server-authoritative physics + on-chain settlement math. Hard to find a team credible in both.
- Track record: ~3-month build, three audits shipped, two fix bundles, working wagered matches on devnet. Joint commit history. Not pitching what we'd build — pitching what already exists.
- Fish on product/strategy/narrative; JJ on engineering. Complementary.

**Strong lines to drop if natural:**
- *"We're not pitching what we'd build if you funded us. We're pitching what we already built."*
- *"Gaming people don't speak crypto. Crypto people don't speak gaming. We do both."*

**What a 35-second natural delivery sounds like:**
> *"Honestly — we're not pitching what we'd build if you funded us. We're pitching what we already built. I grew up on artillery games, Pocket Tanks, Worms. Fish has been deep in the TG side of crypto for years. Gaming people don't speak crypto. Crypto people don't speak gaming. We see the gap because we're in both. Three months of build, three audits, wagered matches settled on devnet — the receipts are in the repo."*

### 4 · "SO WHERE DOES THIS GO?" · 1:30 → 1:55

**JJ pivots naturally** from his "why us" answer. Same breath, no formal hand-off.

**WHERE NEXT — points to hit:**
- SolShot is the wedge. Artillery is the first game.
- The bet: a whole group-chat arcade. Golf, 8-ball, basketball, hockey, darts. Same backend, same wallet, same SHOT economy, same async-turn loop.
- Then multi-platform: same Anchor programs, different chat shell — Seekr Mobile, iMessage, WhatsApp. Combined surface ~5B users.
- Adding a chat surface = an addition, not a rebuild.

**Strong lines to drop if natural:**
- *"We want to monopolise the gap we see. Nobody else is building this."*
- *"Adding a chat surface isn't a rebuild. It's an addition."*

**What a 25-second natural delivery sounds like:**
> *"SolShot's the wedge. Artillery is just the first game. The real bet is a whole group-chat arcade — golf, 8-ball, basketball, hockey, all on the same backend, same wallet, same SHOT economy. Then we ship the same thing on Seekr Mobile, iMessage, WhatsApp. Adding a chat surface isn't a rebuild — it's an addition. We want to monopolise the gap we see."*

**Fish optional riff (~5s) — the moat line:**
> *"Yeah — single-game wagers compete on game quality. Multi-game async wagers across a friend group is infrastructure. That's the moat."*

---

## What CANNOT be in the recording

- ❌ "TAM" / "go-to-market" / "primitives" / "category-defining" / "innovative"
- ❌ Reciting a list of features
- ❌ "As a founder" / "we strongly believe" / "in today's market"
- ❌ Comparison to a specific other project ("we're the [X] of Y")
- ❌ Promises about mainnet date specifics ("we'll launch on June 1st")
- ❌ Numbers that aren't true (don't make up user counts, revenue, etc.)

---

## If one of you freezes

Cover lines either of you can throw out:

- *"Yeah, and the other thing about that…"* → keep going
- *"Sorry let me put it differently…"* → re-state
- *"What do you reckon?"* / *"You take it?"* → hand off, buy 3 seconds

Two-second pauses are fine. Re-recording 6 times to "get it perfect" is what kills the format.
